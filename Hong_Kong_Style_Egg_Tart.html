Egg Puffs

These quintessential Hong Kong street snacks can be found at every night market corner. Named after their unique round shape, these puffs hold a crispy exterior with a soft and chewy interior. Typically enjoyed hot, these can be enjoyed plain, with fruit, and or ice cream. (note. egg puff machine needed)

Ingredients

Yield: 8 servings

Total Time: 1 hour

  • 2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp tapioca starch
  • 2 tbsp custard powder
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 1/3 cup granulated white sugar
  • 4 tbsp evaporated milk
  • 1 1/3 cup water
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract or essence
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (additional oil needed for brushing pans)

Instructions

  1. Combine all ingredients in a large bowl, mix with a whisk until minimal lumps remain.
  2. Place batter in fridge for 40 minutes.
  3. Preheat egg puff machine to medium-high heat. Preheating times may vary depending on machine but typically takes 1-2 minutes.
  4. Brush egg puff machine lightly with oil, ensuring oil coats entire pan.
  5. Place 2/3 cup of batter in the center of the egg puff machine.
  6. Close the lid of the machine and flip immediately, cook for 2 minutes.
  7. Flip the machine again and cook for 2 minutes on the other side. Cooking times may vary but generally takes 2 minutes on each side.
  8. Remove egg puff with a chopstick or fork, gently pulling on the sides that have curled away from the pan.
  9. Repeat steps to cook the remain batter, best served immediately.
