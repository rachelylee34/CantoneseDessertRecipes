Origins Behind Egg Puffs

Egg Puffs are now commonly enjoyed across the world as a popular snack and dessert. However, the story behind egg puffs and its ties to Hong Kong remains an interesting story to tell.

First seen on the streets of Hong Kong in the 1950s, egg puffs were said to be originally created as a way for shop keepers to use their broken eggs that could no longer be sold. Rather than tossing the eggs, a shopkeeper cleverly came up with a way to combine the broken eggs with simple ingredients such as milk and flour to make a batter. Due to its readily available ingredients, vendors made sure this was a cheap dessert that could be enjoyed and shared by with everyone. The egg puff mold was created with holes for miniature bubbles so that family and friends could share this dessert together, easily allowing them to break off individual bubbles. Cooked over a coal fire, these quickly became a staple and can be seen across night markers and street vendors. It's name in Cantonese is 雞蛋仔 (gai daan jai) which directly translates to "little chicken egg."
